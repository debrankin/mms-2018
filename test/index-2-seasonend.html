Maine Music Society Concludes its
2015-16 Season

Greetings from
Artistic Director, John Corrie

he Maine Music Society begins a banner year this fall celebrating 25 years of Making Music Soar!

It is amazing to realize the strength of the organization as well as the incredible number of concerts that the society has provided over the years. Doing the math, we have performed at least 75 concerts in our regular season offerings. We have produced concerts ranging from Bach, Mozart and Brahms to Cole Porter, George Gershwin and the Beatles. In addition, we have participated in so many different community events including the L/A Art Walk, Christmas carols at St. Mary’s Hospital, naturalization ceremonies for our newest citizens, celebrations for film festivals, significant anniversaries associated with the Basilica of Ss. Peter and Paul, Veterans’ Day festivities, and even a wedding anniversary or two. Indeed we are ready, willing and most able to take our music making on the road to add our voices to a celebration.

This season we will present our familiar lineup of concerts: Battle of the Blends in November, A Heritage Christmas in mid-December, a classical music concert in early April, and a popular music concert in mid-May. The “Blends” committee has been busy assembling a wide variety of talent for the Battle of the Blends concert. The Heritage Christmas concert will focus on music of the British Isles. The April concert will be a twin-city-wide collaboration of musical talent presenting the Missa Solemnis by Ludwig van Beethoven. Imagine the orchestra and chorus of the Maine Music Society joining forces with the Bates College Choir and Orchestra, and the Chamber Choirs of both Edward Little and Lewiston High Schools. As in the magnificent performance of the Brahms Requiem in March of 2007, these groups will assemble to perform both Calm Sea and Prosperous Voyage and this momentous Solemn Mass by the most famous composer of the Classical music period. And this time, the newly formed Basilica Schola led by Basilica musician Scott Vaillancourt will join us. We will finish the season with the stunning music of two of my favorite rock musicians/composers/performers, The Piano Men - Billy Joel and Elton John.

There will be music for everyone. Come celebrate with us as we continue the tradition of Making Music Soar.

Sincerely,



Artistic Director

The Maine Music Society Celebrates 25 Years

e invite you to celebrate the 25th anniversary of the Maine Music Society. We incorporated as a non-profit professional organization in 1991 to produce quality choral performances in Central Maine and serve as an institutional umbrella for our choral and orchestral ensembles. Building on the heritage of the Androscoggin Chorale, a community chorus formed in 1972, we have expanded over the years to include a professional orchestra and a small a cappella group. This year we are retiring the name “Androscoggin Chorale” and will be known as the Maine Music Society Chorale.

The Maine Music Society presents four major concerts each year in Lewiston’s Franco Center and other area venues. In addition, we contribute our music to a variety of community events including the Art Walk, Salute to Veterans and naturalization ceremonies for new US citizens. Our commitment to good music, cooperative educational opportunities for young singers, imaginative programming and musical excellence is a source of pride for citizens of Lewiston-Auburn. Under the direction of Artistic Director John Corrie, the Maine Music Society Chorale, Chamber Singers and Orchestra draw talented musicians from many communities near and far who come together to share their love of music and enhance the rich artistic and cultural heritage of Central Maine. Please join us in Making Music Soar!

Highlights of the Year

The Battle of the Blends on November 7 again provided a showcase of exceptional a cappella singing for a sold-out audience. This was followed by our traditional Heritage Christmas celebration featuring the Chorale singing music from the British Isles and a carol sing-along.

The centerpiece of the season was a spectacular collaborative performance of Missa Solemnis and another work by Ludwig van Beethoven at the Basilica of Saints Peter and Paul on April 3.  You can read an in-depth article about this concert here. The Chorale wrapped-up its season in May with Piano Men featuring many favorite catchy and pithy songs by Billy Joel and Elton John.

Our 20 voice a cappela Chamber Singers continued to provide outreach and entertainment at numerous community events throughout the year, including Art Walks, a Naturalization Ceremony for immigrants, the dedication of the new Lewiston amphitheater by the river in Simard-Payne Park, and a concert in Islesboro on July 9.
