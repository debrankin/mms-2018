2014 - 2015 Performance Season

Greetings from
Artistic Director, John Corrie

he Maine Music Society is gearing up for stunning concerts celebrating the Power of Music. Beginning in November we present music’s power to entertain with the Battle of the Blends. The a cappella groups Good-Time Sound, the Merrimanders, None of the Above, the Nor’easters Barbershop Chorus, and Vocal Solution will raise the roof at the Franco Center!

In December, we’ll experience music’s power to celebrate. The Chorale will collaborate with the Oak Hill High School Choir bringing joy to everyone’s holiday with carols of the season, and with a Christmas carol sing along!

In March, the Chorale and the Maine Music Society Orchestra will demonstrate music’s power to transcend with a performance of two Requiems, Gabriel Fauré’s and Maurice Duruflé’s. These beautiful settings of the Requiem texts will move the listeners from the grief of loss to the joy of heavenly promise.

We’ll conclude with music’s power to inspire. 2015 marks the 150th anniversary of the end of the Civil War; the Chorale will perform songs representing both sides with such familiar tunes as Battle Hymn of the Republic and The Yellow Rose of Texas. Well-known local fiddler Greg Boardman will join us with a performance of The Ashokan Farewell. Join us for a season that celebrates the power of music to entertain, to celebrate, to transcend, and to inspire!

Sincerely,



Artistic Director

The Maine Music Society Chamber Singers

he Singers have been performing frequently since they organized over a year ago. This smaller group, made up of members of the Androscoggin Chorale, prepare and sing every song without accompaniment – a cappella – which means that the singers can travel to any location.

Indeed the voices of this smaller group have performed at many different locations: the Naturalization Ceremony for our newest citizens at the Lewiston Public Library in May, 2014; the Art Walk this past June and August; the summer Wednesday Noonday Concert series at the Basilica of Ss Peter and Paul; and the 25th anniversary of the Knights of Columbus from Holy Family, Lewiston.

They sang for the first ever Salute to Veterans last November and will participate once again this November in the second Salute. Their voices have also been raised in concert at the Cumston Hall, in Monmouth.

They’ve been busy! Musical choices stretch from the 16th century right up to the present singing songs by George Gershwin, the Beatles, Billy Joel, and they are even preparing a song by Pentatonix.

Check them out!
